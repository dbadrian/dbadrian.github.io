Introduction

tl;dr: I love fermenting food and I need some technical equipment in order to be efficient (and lazy) when it comes to specialized ferments required specific temperature and humidity

DIY? Oh, that sounds great! An Incubator? Okay, that is odd... Grow mold? Aaaaaand we are back in that weird place on the internet. Not!

Soooo... a couple of months back, I became (heavily) interested in the topic of fermentation. While I always enjoyed cooking, I hardly ever "produced" anything but what I would immediately consume. Never something I could put on the shelf for a couple of weeks or months. For my grandparents it was still normal to produce large quantities of Sauerkraut when winter approached. They would store large amounts of cabbage, salt, and water in a big oak barrel, letting it slowly ferment over the coming months.

While the spirit of fermenting your own food as means of preservation is still alive nowadays (in Asia it is still extremely common), it has somewhat fallen out of practice at home. TODO: WELl there are still other people

Long story short, I radicalized myself in the ancient ways of lacto-acid fermentation and that obsession quickly grew broader and deeper from there. Once I moved past simple lacto-acid fermentation, where it would be sufficient to simply submerge things under (salt)-water and wait [footnote that temp etc. would still influence taste etc.], certain fermentations would require specific conditions with regards to temperature and humidity.

Desired Functionality

This is an engineering project, so obviously I defined some specs beforehand, of what I would like this thing to be able to do:

  1. Hold a specified temperature in the range 30-40 degree Celsius.
    • This should take into account, that the temperature within inoculated {beans, rice, etc.} might be higher.
  2. Follow a predefined temperature/time curve.
  3. Sent notice messages at specified times, to remind user to carry out specific tasks.
  4. Be somewhat power efficient.
  5. Safe to use, as I want to leave it alone for up to 24 hours.
  6. Easy to use - I don't want to change warm bags of water every 8 hours [maybe a footcite here?].
  7. Cheap to build if possible.
  8. Optional: Hold a specific humidity level. Follow humidity curve
